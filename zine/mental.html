Mental

A list of mental activities you can do within your own home.

1. Complete a puzzle: The more pieces the better! Feeling extra saucy? Take on a Rubik's Cube. More of a word person? Crossword puzzle!
2. Start a journal or blog. Sure, it can be about the coronavirus, but it could also be about a specific interest from chess to cheese.
3. Watch all the really long movies you’ve avoided until now.
4. Download Duolingo, or a similar app, and teach yourself a foreign language.
5. Look at pictures of puppies.
6. Finally read “Infinite Jest,” “Les Miserables” or even “The Stand.” Go all in and read “Ulysses.” You got this.
7. Meditate. Try lying down with your eyes closed, palms up and while focusing on your breath. Or spend 20 minutes sitting crosslegged and repeat a soothing word to yourself in your head. (The latter is more like transcendental meditation.)
8. Put on a soap opera. Mute the sound. Create your own dialogue.
9. Watch the films that won Oscars for best picture.
10. Watch films that won Independent Spirit Awards for best picture.
11. Watch films that critics say should have won those aforementioned awards.
12. Read all the New Yorker issues piled on your desk.
13. Will Tom Hanks into recovery from coronavirus by watching every Tom Hanks movie chronologically.
14. Actually try to reproduce something you see on Pinterest. Probably fail. Try again.
15. Pretend you're 13 years old and fold a square piece of paper into a fortune teller you put your thumbs and pointer fingers into. Proceed to tell fortunes.
16. Go through your camera roll, pick your favorite pics from the past year and make a photo book or order framed versions online.
17. Make a list of things for which you are grateful.
18. Have your own wine tasting of whatever bottles you have at home. Make up stories about the journey of the grapes to your mouth.
19. Work on your financial planning, such as exploring whether to refinance your loan or ways to save more money.
20. Read Robert Jordan’s 14-book “Wheel of Time” series before it streams on Amazon starring Rosamund Pike.
21. Make lists of all the museums, sporting events and concerts you want to visit when they finally reopen.
22. Get into comics with digital subscriptions on your tablet, like Marvel Unlimited.
23. Memorize the periodic table. You never know when that will come in handy.
24. Get a free trial of a streaming service and binge-watch as much as you can before it expires.
25. Apply for a new job. You have remote work experience now.
26. Update or write your will and organize your affairs. Yes, it sounds melodramatic and morbid but let’s face it: This is a task many of us avoid because we never have the time. Now we do.
27. Watch the "Star Wars" movies in this and only this order: Rogue One-IV-V-II-III-Solo-VI-VII-VIII-IX.
28. Two words: Coronavirus beard! Grow it, moisturize it, comb it, love it.
29. Learn the words to "Tung Twista." Get them so ingrained in your brain that you can rap them as fast as Twista can. Impress everyone.
30. Prepare to verbally duel a bully who wants to discuss the evolution of the market economy in the Southern colonies, by memorizing Matt Damon's "Good Will Hunting" speech.
31. Try to speak in pig Latin. Or, "ig-pay, atin-Lay."
32. Talk to your plants. How are they doing? Make sure they are getting the amount of sunlight they should be. Check their soil. Water if necessary.
33. Consider donating money to food banks to help families struggling to get meals.
34. Write a song. If you want to make it about your time inside and put it to the tune of "My Sharona" and replace "Sharona" with "Corona," do what you have to do.
36. Study the art of beatboxing.
37. Learn Old English words. Pepper them into your conversation. Wherefore not?.
38. Take deep breaths, in through your nose and out through your mouth.
39. Sleep. Get lots of it.
