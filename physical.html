Physical

A list of physical activities you can do within your own home.

52. If it won't bother your neighbors: Dust off that old instrument and practice.
53. Face masks, moisturizer, oh my! Treat yourself to a 10-step skin care routine you don’t have time for during a normal work week.
54. Put together the most attractive charcuterie board possible, but you can only use foods you already have in your fridge and cupboard.
55. Take note from "Tangled" star Rapunzel, who has an entire song about how she's spent her days alone in a castle. Activities included in her ditty: Ventriloquy, candle-making, papier-mâché and adding a new painting to her gallery.
56. Learn calligraphy. YouTube can help.
57. Have a space in your home where all of the tupperware goes? Organize it and actually match lids to containers.
58. Try on all your clothes and determine whether they “spark joy” á la Marie Kondo.
59. Better yet, go through this process with your junk drawer and supply shelves.
60. Knit or crochet.
61. Try out at-home aerobics or yoga videos. Consider downloading a fitness app with curated workout playlists.
62. Look at yourself in the mirror. Attempt a self portrait with pencil and paper.
63. Take a bubble bath (bonus: Add a glass of wine).
64. Make a classic cocktail, from negronis to Manhattans and aperol spritzes. Don't forget the garnish.
65. Coloring books: They’re not just for kids.
66. Take time to reflect: What have you accomplished in the last year? What goals are you setting for yourself in the next year?
67. Write a short story or get started on that novel.
68. Clear out the family room and camp indoors with all blankets, popcorn and scary movies.
69. Finally get around to fixing that broken door knob and loose tile or cleaning scuffed up walls.
70. Acquire a foam roller and treat yourself to some physical therapy.
71. Learn how to braid (fishtail, French, etc.) via YouTube tutorial..
72. Throw out all your too-old makeup and products. (Tip: most liquid products have a small symbol on them noting expirations, usually six months to a year. This includes sunscreen!)
73. Go on a health kick and learn how to cook new recipes with ingredients you may not be using already, from miso to tahini.
74. Perfect grandma’s bolognese recipe.
75. Make coffee, but this time study how many beans you use, which types, how hot the water is, how long it brews and whether any of that makes a difference.
76. Buy gift cards from your favorite local businesses to help keep them in business while we quarantine.
77. Watch “Frozen 2,’ which went up early on Disney Plus. Another new movie on the streaming service: "Stargirl."
78. Get into baking with "The Great British Baking Show," but your technical challenge is baking something with the ingredients you have on hand (that you didn't already use in the charcuterie board).
79. Indoor scavenger hunt.
80. Go viral in the good way by making a quarantine-themed TikTok.
81. Rearrange your sock drawer. Really.
82. Stop procrastinating and do your income taxes.
83. Rearrange your furniture to make it seem like your home is a totally different space.
84. Practice shuffling playing cards like a Poker dealer. Be ready for employment opportunities once all casinos open back up.
85. Organize your spice rack alphabetically or get crazy and do it by cuisine.
86. Teach your dog to shake. Hand sanitizer optional.
87. Order and put together some IKEA furniture. Time yourself.
88. Learn a new style of dance via YouTube, from bellydancing to breaking.
89. The parades have been canceled but you can still make corned beef and cabbage for St. Patrick’s Day.
90. Bring out the Legos. Build your house inside of your house.
91. Attempt things with your non-dominant hand, from writing to brushing your teeth. Prepare to be frustrated.
92. How many words per minute can you type? See if you can get speedier by taking a typing course.
93. Learn origami. Make cranes for your loved ones.
94. Stretch. Work on your flexibility. It's possible to get the splits back, right?
95. Deep condition your hair and put paraffin wax on your hands. Enjoy your soft hair and nails.
96. Try moving in super-slow motion. It's OK to laugh at regular speed.
97. You know how there are dozens of ways to wear a scarf, but you only wear it the one way? Learn the other ways.
98. Apply for a new job. You have remote work experience now.
99. Go for a walk outside at least once per day, for at least 20 minutes at a time.
100. Plant a garden for vegetables, for flowers, or a mix of the two.
