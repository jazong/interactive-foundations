Birdwatching 101

A simple guide to birdwatching in Hong Kong.

Hong Kong is a great place for birds. Although there are no endemics - the land area is too small, the connection to the Chinese mainland too close. Its position in south China on  a main migration route for birds moving between East Asia and South-east Asia makes it an exciting place for the bird watchers and bird photographers who live here - and for birders from overseas who visit or pass through on their own personal migrations.

The total land area is just over 1100 square kilometres - smaller than Greater London in the UK - but there are currently 555 species on the Hong Kong list, which is an impressive number for such a small, densely populated territory.

Here is a schedule that shows the migration periods of 6 rare bird species: the Black-faced Spoonbill; the Saunders's Gull; the Eastern Imperial Eagle; the Asian Dowitchers; the Nordmann's Greenshank and last but not least the Spoon-billed Sandpiper.
